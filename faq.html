WHAT IS USDT

USDT, or Tether, is a type of cryptocurrency known as a stablecoin that was launched in 2014. It is designed to maintain a stable value by being pegged to a traditional fiat currency, typically the United States dollar (USD), at a 1:1 ratio. This means that for every USDT token in circulation, there should be an equivalent amount of USD held in reserve. The primary purpose of USDT is to provide stability and facilitate transactions within the cryptocurrency ecosystem. It aims to address the volatility often associated with other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum, which can experience significant price fluctuations in short periods.

HOW IT IS WORKS?

To understand how USDT works, let's dive into its creation and operation: Issuance: Tether Limited, the company behind USDT, mints new tokens by issuing them on the chosen blockchain network. Initially, these tokens are not in circulation but rather held by Tether Limited. Reserve: To back the value of USDT, Tether Limited claims to hold an equivalent amount of fiat currency, typically USD, in designated bank accounts. These reserves are audited to provide transparency and assure users that the value of USDT is backed by real-world assets. Tokenization: Once the fiat reserves are verified, Tether Limited converts the corresponding amount of USD into USDT tokens.
